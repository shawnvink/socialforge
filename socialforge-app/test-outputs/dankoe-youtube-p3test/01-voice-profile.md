### Sentence Architecture

**Pattern 1: Problem-Agitation Statement** (40% of sentences)
```
Structure: [Most people] + [do negative behavior] + [negative consequence]
Examples:
  - "Most people don't know how to learn because they fry their brain with podcasts and YouTube videos"
  - "When you watch endless tutorials, you fill your mind with noise and chaos"
  - "Most people have 16 waking hours and waste 12 of them doing things that don't matter"
```

**Pattern 2: Direct Challenge/Command** (30% of sentences)
```
Structure: [Imperative verb] + [specific action]
Examples:
  - "Stop trying to find your passion"
  - "Go acquire it"
  - "Put it on your calendar"
  - "Create an aim for your learning"
```

**Pattern 3: Provocative Question** (15% of sentences)
```
Structure: Question that assumes negative behavior
Examples:
  - "Why are you even learning in the first place if not to do something with it?"
  - "How much you learn is directly correlated with how much progress you make on the project"
```

**Pattern 4: Personal Credibility Insertion** (10% of sentences)
```
Structure: "I" + [past struggle] or "I" + [current method]
Examples:
  - "When I want to learn something new, I don't start learning"
  - "Personally, writing changed my life"
  - "I was once a freelance web designer who failed at every business"
```

**Pattern 5: Aphorism/Principle Statement** (5% of sentences)
```
Structure: Short declarative principle, often using colon
Examples:
  - "Learning comes from struggle, not memorization"
  - "Passion follows mastery, not the other way around"
  - "The mark of a free person is that they learn how to learn"
```

### Argumentative Structure

**Thesis Pattern**:
1. Identify what "most people" do wrong
2. State the negative consequence
3. Present the harsh truth/alternative
4. Give direct command for correction
5. (Optional) Insert personal credibility example

**Example**:
```
Most people [problem] → 
"And six months later, after learning so much, they have nothing to show for it" [consequence] → 
"Here's the harsh truth — you're learning slow" [reframe] → 
"Outline the project first" [command] → 
"When I want to learn something new, I don't start learning" [credibility]
```
